Data Introduction

Objective

- Build a regression model to predict the probability of rain based on meteorological factors.

Research question

- This study aims to examine which meteorological factors significantly affect daily precipitation in Hanoi from 2015 to 2024.

- Specifically, it investigates how temperature, humidity, wind speed, cloud cover, precipitation probability, and visibility contribute to variations in rainfall amounts.

Data Source: Kaggle
About Dataset
Dataset

- The data in the study were collected from Kaggle Data for the Hanoi area, period 2015–2024.

- The selected variables directly reflect meteorological factors that are likely to affect daily rainfall in Hanoi.

Data description
  • precip ( mm): Average total rainfall per day .
  • temp ( °C): Average daily temperature.
  • humidity ( %): The level of water vapor in the air.
  • windspeed ( km/h): Average wind intensity during the day.
  • precipprob ( %): Forecast probability of rain during the day.
  • visibility ( km): Average viewing distance during the day

Meaning of model coefficients
Meaning of coefficients

Regression results after using Newey-West, other factors remain constant:

  • - humidity: When humidity increases by 1%, expected rainfall increases by about 0.046 mm, keeping other factors constant.
  • - visibility: When visibility decreases by 1 km, expected rainfall increases by about 0.19 mm.
  • - windspeed: When wind speed increases by 1 km/h, expected rainfall increases by about 0.011 mm.
  • - precipprob: When the probability of forecasted rain increases by 1%, expected rainfall increases by 0.006 mm.
  • - temp: When temperature (temp) increases by 1°C, predicted rainfall decreases by 0.01 mm.

Conclusion and Interpretation
Conclusion and Interpretation

The model has an Adjusted R² = 30.8%, meaning that the meteorological variables in the model only explain about 30.8% of the rainfall variation in Hanoi.

Despite the low level of explanation, the overall regression is still statistically significant (F-test p < 0.001), showing that the included variables have a real relationship with rainfall, not a random one.

This is reasonable because rainfall is affected by many complex atmospheric processes (pressure, monsoon, convection, ENSO...) that are not in the data, so the model only explains a small part of the actual variation.
